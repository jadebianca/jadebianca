Fill Up Your Own Cup and Let Them Fall in Love With the Overflow

By: Jade Bianca Portillo • January 19, 2026

Intro • Stayed Selfless • Peace in Solitude • Choosing Myself

Sometimes I feel like the world only notices what I do, not who I am. People only focus on my achievements, but rarely ask how I am really doing. As the first-born daughter in my family, I grew up carrying silent expectations. I was judged more than understood and often left out. Still, I kept asking myself: If I don't choose and love myself, then who will?

Stayed Selfless Despite Being Misunderstood

I was often misunderstood, yet I cared deeply for them and tried to put their needs before my own. I've experienced favoritism and spent years trying to please people just to be noticed. My feelings were often ignored and, I was once told I couldn't be tired because other had it worst. Because of this, I became afraid to open up my feelings, even to my closest friends. I always listened to them, but I feared of being a burden. I cared deeply, yet people still misunderstood me as maka-sarili or manhid. Finding Peace in Solitude

There were times when I put myself on DND because I needed time to be with myself and recharge. I started enjoying solo dates and felt free when I am all alone. Being away from home made me feel more at peace and more like myself. I even even get shy when all the attention is on me, even though I spent years craving for it. In solitude, I found healing through simple moments like:

  • Walking and exploring alone
  • Eating alone
  • Sitting quietly with my own my thoughts

In those moments, I stopped begging to be chosen and began filling my own cup little by little.

Back to top ↑

Choosing Myself Without Guilt

Now I understand that choosing myself is not selfish. Filling my own cup has taught me how to choose and love without losing myself. I no longer need to prove my worth just to be seen. The right people will naturally notice and appreciate my love, energy, and effort. This is me choosing myself and finally letting that be enough.
